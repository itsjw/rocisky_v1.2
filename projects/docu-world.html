Embrace the Documentary World

A concept design of documentary video mobile app

side project collaborated with Tina Wang two weeks

Problem Space

People love watching videos. Youtube, Netflix and many more new video services have changed how and what people watched everyday in recent years. However, documentary videos have been less covered in these new platforms. Compared with movies and TV programs, documentary videos has much less population. But there are always people interested in them and lots of great documentary videos are in lack of exposure. With the trending that people watch videos more often on smartphones during leisure time, documentary videos, usually shorter and less addictive, could be a good candidate for such mobile users to consume.

Target Users

DocuWorld is targeting at mobile users with two major scenarios. The first scenario is for people who love documentary or work in this industry. They usually have their favorites topics or authors and would like to keep update with the most recent videos. Sometimes, they will dig into the topic or author so they can find more interesting contents. The second scenario is for general public who know less of documentary videos but have some fragment time to watch something. While sitting in a bus or waiting for the train, they pull up their smartphones to watch something hot at the moment.

Concept Design

DocuWorld is designed for better recommending documentary videos for users to cultivate their interests in documentary world. The design goal is to make it really easy for users to quickly find something great to watch. There are three feeds available according to different use cases. The home feed recommends videos based on topics and authors user followed. The hot feed simply lists documentary videos based on population in different timeframes. The event feed provides hand picked videos grouped into collection that is relevant to what is happening around the world. For example, an olympic collection can be presented while the world’s attention is on Rio 2016. On top of all three feeds, a time filter is available to help user select videos short enough to watch in their mobile contexts.
